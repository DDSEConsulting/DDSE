What the Patent Covers

Data Driven Computing System Development

U.S. Patent 12,197,893 B2 claims a method of constructing computing system software based on a single, universal insight: all data in a computing system can be completely and exclusively described by three types and two systematic attributes. This minimal, precise framework is the foundation for every safety goal, safety criterion, and software construction activity in the patent.

The Three Types of Data
Every data element in any computing system falls into exactly one of these three categories — no more, no less.
Input
Input Data — Imported from outside Data received by the system from external sources: sensor readings, network signals, user commands, bus messages (CAN, LIN, Ethernet). Defined by the input transmission protocol.
Middle
Middle Data — Stored internally Configuration, calibration, and state data generated during calculations and stored in memory devices (NVM, RAM, Flash). Persists across operations and power cycles.
Output
Output Data — Exported to outside Data derived from calculations and transmitted out of the system to actuators, displays, or communication networks via the output transmission protocol.
"Consisting only of three types of data: input, middle and output." — Claim 1[b][ii]
The Two Systematic Attributes
Each of the three data types is characterized by exactly two safety-relevant attributes — and only two.

Data Value Attribute

The content of the data — what it represents. For example: vehicle speed = 72 km/h, brake pressure = 3.7 bar, gear position = 3. Safety mechanisms must ensure this value is correct, within range, plausible, and uncorrupted.

Data Timing Attribute

The temporal property of the data — when it arrives or is processed. For example: deadline adherence, timestamp, worst-case delay, execution window. Safety mechanisms must ensure data is available on time and processed within defined constraints.

"Wherein each said data consists of only two systematic attributes consisting of data value attribute and data timing attribute." — Claim 1[b][i]

All safety requirements in ISO 26262, AUTOSAR, IEC 61508 and related standards — CRC checks, watchdogs, plausibility checks, deadline monitoring, E2E protection, ASIL decomposition — exist solely to preserve, validate, or recover one or both of these two attributes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Licensing FAQ

Common questions from companies evaluating their licensing obligations under U.S. Patent 12,197,893 B2.

Does implementing AUTOSAR or ISO 26262 mean we practice this patent?+
Under Federal Circuit precedent (Fujitsu v. Netgear, 620 F.3d 1321), compliance with a technical standard can establish practice when the standard necessarily requires practicing each element of a patent claim. Both ISO 26262 and AUTOSAR mandate software construction methods that inherently implement the three data types and two systematic attributes claimed in the patent. If your system is certified or compliant with these standards, it is very likely practicing the patented method.
The standards predate the patent. Does that invalidate it?+
No. Under KSR v. Teleflex (550 U.S. 398) and Diamond v. Diehr (450 U.S. 175), a patent is valid if it claims a specific, non-obvious improvement or implementation not taught by prior art. ISO 26262 and AUTOSAR define detailed requirements but do not teach or suggest the specific first-principle insight of the '893 patent: that ALL computing system data can be completely and exclusively classified into exactly three types, each characterized by exactly two systematic attributes. That unifying framework is the novel contribution.
We received a pre-suit notice. What should we do?+
We recommend consulting with IP counsel and reaching out to DDSE Consulting promptly. Companies that respond in good faith and initiate licensing discussions are eligible for the Early Adopter discount of up to 50%. Continued implementation without a license after receipt of actual notice may expose your company to enhanced damages for willful infringement under 35 U.S.C. § 284.
What does "non-exclusive" license mean for our business?+
A non-exclusive license grants your organization the right to practice the patented method in your products without restriction on DDSE Consulting also licensing other parties. You are not acquiring exclusivity over the technology — you are obtaining legal authorization to use it. All licenses offered are non-exclusive and worldwide, covering your implementations under all applicable standards.
What industries and products are covered?+
The patent covers any computing system software construction method that classifies data into input, middle, and output types with data value and data timing attributes — regardless of industry. This includes but is not limited to: automotive ECUs, industrial PLCs, medical device software, aerospace avionics, agricultural control systems, and any safety-critical embedded system implementing IEC 61508, ISO 26262, AUTOSAR, ISO 25119, IEC 62061, or derived standards.
How does the FRAND commitment affect licensing terms?+
DDSE Consulting's FRAND commitment means licensing terms are offered on a non-discriminatory basis — similarly situated licensees receive comparable terms. "Fair and reasonable" means royalty rates that reflect the patent's contribution to the standard, consistent with industry practice for standard-essential patents. The 50% early adopter discount does not violate FRAND obligations because it is available to all parties that proactively engage before enforcement proceedings.
